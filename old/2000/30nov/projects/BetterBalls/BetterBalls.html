This is the Better Balls project. It is my first project that I actually feel is worthwhile to put up a major explanation page.

Better Balls is, I think, a natural step that every games programmer takes when first starting out... they make circles, and then try to bounce them off of each other. My friend (who works at a large game company), said that a lot of programmers looked at the program running on his screen at work with a whistful look in their eye.

Click the screenshot for a closer look.

While I was working on this, I used George Foot's Allegro Vivace Tutorial. In particular, I used his basic setup for a 'game', and his code for circles. (see chapter 4 in the tutorial). I figured that that part was the simplest part, since I'd already drawn primitive shapes and made them move.

The circles are all pseudo-randonly generated: their x,y coordinates, their velocity vector, their colour and their radius are all genereated using rand(). However, there is code in place that determines if the circles are generated over top of each other. (See collisions.c). I was able to reuse the same code to detect collisions.

Problems with this current build: the physics is hacky. To make them bounce, all I do is switch velocity vectors between circles. As well, circles will get stuck together, and will disappear off of the screen. I'm working on a trick or two to fix this.
